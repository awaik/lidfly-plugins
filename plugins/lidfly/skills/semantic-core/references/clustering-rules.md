# Clustering Rules

Build clusters for a useful page or campaign structure, not for lexical neatness.

## Keep Together

Keep phrases in one cluster when they share:

- one user intent and stage of demand;
- one relevant landing page;
- compatible offer, geography, and economics;
- ad copy or page content that can naturally answer every phrase.

Avoid SKAG by default. Auto-strategies usually need enough combined data to learn.

## Split

Split phrases when they require:

- different landing pages or product categories;
- different geography, delivery, or availability;
- different value of conversion, budget, or strategy;
- different offers or legal wording;
- separate brand, competitor, generic, informational, or support intent.

Use observed search results only as supporting evidence. If no SERP source is available, mark the split as an intent hypothesis rather than inventing result similarity.

## Campaign Boundaries

Usually separate:

- search and networks;
- brand, competitors, and generic demand;
- regions with different economics;
- product categories or services with different margins;
- campaigns that need independent budgets, goals, or landing pages.

Apply cross-negatives only after the final structure is known. Do not block a valuable long-tail phrase merely because two clusters share one word.
